                      LaTeX Equation Help

                      Use LaTeX to write mathematical equations in your courses.

                      Inline Equations (in text)

                      Surround with single dollar signs: $E = mc^2$

                      Result: $E = mc^2$

                      Display Equations (on separate line)

                      Surround with double dollar signs: $$E = mc^2$$

                      Result: $$E = mc^2$$

                      Common Examples

                      • $E = hf$ ‚Üí Einstein's photoelectric equation
                      • $\lambda = \frac{h}{p}$ ‚Üí de Broglie wavelength
                      • $\Delta x \cdot \Delta p \geq \frac{h}{4\pi}$ ‚Üí Uncertainty principle
                      • $$\int_0^\infty e^{-x} dx = 1$$ ‚Üí Integral example
                      • $x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}$ ‚Üí Quadratic formula

                      Useful Symbols

                      • \alpha, \beta, \gamma ‚Üí Greek letters
                      • \frac{a}{b} ‚Üí Fractions
                      • ^{2} ‚Üí Superscript (exponents)
                      • _{i} ‚Üí Subscript
                      • \sqrt{x} ‚Üí Square root
                      • \sum, \int, \prod ‚Üí Math symbols

                      Tip: Test your LaTeX at latex4technics.com
